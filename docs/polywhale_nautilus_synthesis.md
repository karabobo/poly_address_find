# Polywhale + Nautilus Synthesis

This document compares `DevAntsa/polywhale` and `tentenone1/nautilus-trading` for a paper-driven Polymarket copy-trading research system.

Local snapshots inspected:

- `./polywhale`
- `./nautilus-trading`

## Short Read

Use `polywhale` as the research and validation blueprint. Use `nautilus-trading` as the production execution shell.

`polywhale` is closer to the two papers' research discipline: it separates external whale quality from our captured copy PnL, measures friction, performs walk-forward validation, has survival scoring, and keeps real money gated behind paper evidence.

`nautilus-trading` has a stronger full trading-node shape: NautilusTrader integration, sandbox/live split, crash guards, failover/deployment wiring, dashboard/service scaffolding, and many strategy modules.

The combined target should be:

```text
polywhale discovery + vetting + friction + walk-forward
  -> paper-driven leader score / review queue
  -> NautilusTrader paper execution
  -> only then live eligibility
```

## What Polywhale Does Well

### 1. Friction-first validation

`polywhale/src/polywhale/friction_observer.py` records hypothetical real entry/exit prices from order book snapshots and compares paper PnL with real executable PnL.

This directly addresses the copy-trading paper's key practical issue: the measured copy premium is only about 1.6 percentage points, so slippage and fees can erase it.

Adopt into Nautilus:

- store paper fill price and executable best bid/ask at signal time;
- compute paper-to-real edge retention per signal, wallet, category, and market;
- block live eligibility if edge retention is weak.

### 2. Our-captured-PnL whale review

`polywhale/src/polywhale/whale_review.py` correctly says external win rate tells whether the whale makes money, but our paper PnL tells whether we can capture that money.

This is a major improvement over static `alpha_score`.

Adopt into Nautilus:

- review wallets by settled copy trades generated by the bot;
- keep/drop/boost using our out-of-sample copy results, not leaderboard rank;
- never auto-promote a wallet from external metrics alone.

### 3. Quality gate from reconstructed historical episodes

`polywhale/src/polywhale/whale_vet.py` reconstructs historical position episodes and evaluates:

- resolved sample size;
- total PnL;
- real win rate;
- profit/loss ratio;
- recovery factor;
- max drawdown;
- skew.

This is closer to the "wallet quality" layer we need than a simple Polydata leaderboard import.

Adopt into Nautilus:

- before adding a whale to paper trading, require historical episode metrics;
- do not rely on redeem-only win-rate proxies;
- use recovery factor and profit/loss ratio to reject fragile wallets.

### 4. Walk-forward validation

`polywhale/src/polywhale/walk_forward.py` ranks whales on one training window and follows them blindly on the next unseen window.

This is exactly the right shape for avoiding overfit. It also matches the copy-trading paper's spirit: measure whether imitation outperforms in held-out conditions.

Adopt into Nautilus:

- before live trading, run rolling train/test validation;
- report consistency across windows, not just aggregate PnL;
- paper-approved wallets should expire unless revalidated periodically.

### 5. Sizing discipline

`polywhale/src/polywhale/whale_sizing.py` uses fractional Kelly, sample shrinkage, portfolio caps, per-whale caps, category caps, and category-specific fees.

This is stronger than fixed-size copying.

Adopt into Nautilus:

- exploration stake for low-sample wallets;
- 1/4 Kelly only after enough closed copy trades;
- category-specific fee assumptions;
- explicit caps for per-bet, per-whale, per-category, and total deployed capital.

### 6. Low-latency watch design

`polywhale/src/polywhale/ws_dispatcher.py` uses WebSocket market trade events to trigger targeted wallet snapshots. It does not know who traded from the WebSocket event, but it resnapshots watched wallets holding that asset.

This is pragmatic and useful for following position diffs faster than polling.

Adopt into Nautilus:

- keep timer polling as a safety net;
- add event-triggered resnapshot for markets currently held by watched whales;
- measure detection latency per signal.

## What Nautilus Does Well

### 1. Production trading-node architecture

`nautilus-trading/run_paper.py` wires live Polymarket data into NautilusTrader with sandbox execution. It has explicit paper-mode environment guards and crash handling.

Keep from Nautilus:

- NautilusTrader adapter structure;
- sandbox/live separation;
- process crash guards and health files;
- deployment/failover operational thinking.

### 2. Rich strategy pipeline

Nautilus has many specialized strategy modules under `strategies/`, including edge scoring, position management, market data checks, circuit breakers, shadow ledger, and reporting.

Keep from Nautilus:

- strategy composition;
- circuit breakers;
- paper/live boundary;
- dashboard/reporting if already working.

### 3. Existing scoring and classification hooks

`strategies/wf_edge_scorer.py` already tries to replace an anti-predictive legacy edge score with whale action, category trust, and historical category performance.

Improve it with polywhale and paper methods:

- replace static category weights with measured paper-to-real edge retention;
- add copy graph evidence;
- add event-level vs trade-level win-rate gap;
- add DCA/low-sell resolution holder signals;
- penalize one-off headline wins and pure HFT bots.

## Where Both Still Need Paper-Driven Upgrades

### 1. Copy-leader graph

Neither inspected project fully implements the copy-trading paper's leader/follower identification:

- follower trades same market and direction after leader;
- 1 block window;
- at least 5 co-trades;
- at least 5 markets;
- leader always precedes follower in shared markets;
- 90% in-window containment.

Add this as a separate offline research pipeline. Its output should be a `leader_graph_features` table, not an immediate trading signal.

Recommended columns:

```text
leader_wallet
leader_in_degree
copy_event_count
copy_market_count
median_lag_blocks
containment_pct_median
copy_stream_roi
followers_positive_roi_pct
last_copy_event_at
```

### 2. Resolution-edge holder features

The smart-money paper's strongest signal is resolution-edge holding:

- high event-level win rate;
- lower trade-level win rate;
- DCA entries per market;
- low sell rate;
- politics or slow-resolution category focus;
- likely-human / semi-bot execution rather than pure bot.

Add these to both projects' whale scoring. Polywhale has some historical episode metrics, but it should explicitly add event-level win rate, trade-level win rate, DCA entries, and sell rate.

### 3. Anti-hedge / anti-arbitrage filter

The first paper does not fully rule out hedging. Polywhale has `retail_copyable` and archetype flags, but this should become data-driven.

Add flags:

```text
same_event_offsetting_exposure
net_to_gross_exposure
correlated_market_opposite_exposure
single_leg_copy_risk
spread_capture_pnl_share
```

Simple rule:

```text
if net_to_gross_exposure < 0.35:
    block simple copy trading unless both legs can be reproduced
```

### 4. Data hygiene before leader scoring

The copy-trading paper removes:

- routing/wrap operators;
- wash trades;
- market-maker taker activity.

Polywhale has maker share as a survival placeholder and maker routing, but not the full paper hygiene layer. Nautilus currently appears to rely more on API/leaderboard data and local score files.

Add a pre-score hygiene gate:

```text
not_routing_operator
not_wash_cluster
not_market_maker_taker
maker_fraction <= 0.5 for directional leader scoring
```

## Recommended Combined Roadmap

### Phase 1: Normalize the candidate library

Keep `data/candidate_addresses.csv` as the canonical address library. Add fields from both systems:

```text
address
source
labels
manual_notes
polywhale_watchlist_status
nautilus_alpha_score
candidate_stage
last_reviewed_at
```

Manual addresses stay `needs_data`.

### Phase 2: Build wallet feature store

Compute offline features:

- external PnL and volume;
- recent 30-day volume;
- event/trade win rates;
- DCA and sell rate;
- historical episode PnL;
- recovery factor and drawdown;
- maker/taker share;
- bot/HFT timing features;
- copy graph features;
- hedge/arbitrage flags.

### Phase 3: Score with transparent components

Use `config/leader_scoring_policy.json` as the policy layer, but extend it with polywhale fields:

- `captured_copy_roi`;
- `edge_retention_pct`;
- `walk_forward_consistency_pct`;
- `survival_score`;
- `quality_gate_passed`;
- `retail_copyable`.

### Phase 4: Paper-only promotion

A wallet can enter paper trading only if:

- hygiene passes;
- quality gate passes;
- leader score is above threshold;
- not blocked by hedge/arbitrage flags;
- enough market depth exists for intended stake.

### Phase 5: Live eligibility

Live eligibility should require:

- minimum closed paper trades;
- positive paper ROI after estimated real friction;
- positive walk-forward results;
- bounded drawdown;
- stable recent activity;
- no unresolved hygiene or copyability flags.

## What To Borrow Immediately

From `polywhale` into the Nautilus-style project:

- friction observer;
- whale review by our captured PnL;
- historical episode vetting;
- walk-forward validation;
- fractional Kelly with shrinkage and caps;
- WebSocket-triggered targeted resnapshot;
- archetype / retail-copyable gate.

From `nautilus-trading` into a polywhale-style system:

- NautilusTrader sandbox/live execution shell;
- stronger crash/process guards;
- dashboard and failover deployment ideas;
- richer strategy composition and circuit breaker integration.

From the papers into both:

- copy-leader graph identification;
- event win rate vs trade win rate gap;
- DCA/low-sell resolution holder score;
- routing/wash/MM-taker hygiene;
- explicit anti-hedge and anti-arbitrage filters.

## Bottom Line

`polywhale` is the better research-grade whale evaluation system. `nautilus-trading` is the better production trading shell.

The right combined project should not start by copying live trades. It should first build a strict funnel:

```text
manual / leaderboard / copy-graph candidates
  -> hygiene filters
  -> paper-driven leader score
  -> paper execution with friction measurement
  -> walk-forward validation
  -> live eligibility
```
